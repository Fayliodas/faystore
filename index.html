About Hiki

I was someone who lost their sense of direction. Not in a dramatic way, but quietly slowly drifting away from the paths that once felt certain.

Instead of searching for signs or asking for guidance, I chose to step into uncertainty. I decided to sail through an ocean of questions alone, letting doubt become my compass and silence my companion. Every wave carried a question, and every storm forced me to listen to myself.

I stopped looking for ready made answers. I learned to create them.

Each decision was mine to make, each consequence mine to face. There was no one to blame, no one to impress, and no need to look back. What mattered was moving forward, even when the horizon disappeared.

I don't claim to have everything figured out. I only know that I walk this path by choice with my own answers, my own decisions, and without regretting anything.
